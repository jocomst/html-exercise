Cost of living info

The rental landscape in North Carolina depends on your location within the state. As a result, rent prices and rental opportunities can vary greatly depending on the locale you've set your sights on. You're much less likely to find rental opportunities in some of the more rural locales of the state than you would in one of its major cities. The good news is that the cost of living in North Carolina is relatively average, making it an affordable place to live. It's nowhere near the cost of some of the more notoriously expensive cities (looking at you, San Francisco), but it's also not the least costly place to live either. That said, the COVID pandemic had a significant impact on rental prices in North Carolina, though the state is largely rebounding. Currently, median rent prices are on the rise across the state, Greensboro leading the pack with a 9.5% increase reflected in a year-over-year cost comparison. As prices continue to rise, those looking to save on rent should make their move now. Consider straying away from the newer luxury high-rises to avoid higher rent prices. You might also consider renting with a roommate to cut your expenses in half.
